Research Interests

Optical Transient Type II and Type Ia Supernova

This project began as a summer fellowship with the Dunlap Institute for Astronomy and Astrophysics and later became the focus of my thesis for my HBSc degree. The intriguing phenomenon under study has been shown to be a rare occurrence in core-collapse supernovae. A manuscript is currently in preparation.

I've also worked on a systematic analysis of the early multi-band light curves and colors of 19 Type Ia Supernovae. Our findings revealed three distinct populations of Type Ia SNe based on their color evolution and other characteristics, suggesting potential differences in their production channels.
